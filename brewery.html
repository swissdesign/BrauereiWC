Why Cans? A No‑Brainer.

Cans are the most misunderstood upgrade in craft beer. They are better for the planet and better for your beer — period.

Sustainability

  • Lower transport emissions: lighter and stackable → fewer trucks per litre.
  • Highest recycling efficiency: aluminium is infinitely recyclable with strong EU/CH collection loops.
  • Less breakage & waste: cans don’t shatter and need less protective packaging.
  • Compact storage: reduced warehouse footprint and energy per unit.

Quality & Freshness

  • No light‑strike: zero UV exposure prevents skunky off‑flavours.
  • Lower oxygen pickup: modern canning lines minimise dissolved O₂ for longer shelf life.
  • Faster chilling: aluminium cools quicker for perfect serving temperature.
  • Portable & safe: cans go anywhere — trails, events, poolside — with no glass risks.

Bottles look classic. But if you care about flavour stability and footprint, cans win in every real‑world metric. That’s why our flagship range is canned on site within hours of packaging approval.

Consistency without Compromise

A fully automated brewhouse handles precision steps repeatably — temperatures, timings, transfers. The art stays where it belongs: in recipe design, ingredient selection, and sensory calibration. Automation reduces variance so each batch tastes exactly as intended.

Andermatt Water: A Blank Canvas

Our alpine water is remarkably soft — essentially “empty.” That’s ideal for brewing because we can mineralise with precision (Ca, Mg, Cl⁻, SO₄²⁻) to match each beer style. Instead of fighting hard water (too much calcium or sodium), we build the profile we want, every time.
